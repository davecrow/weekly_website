About me

I am a former history teacher with my sights set on becoming a web developer. To that end, I will be attending Dev Bootcamp in the summer.

When I'm not writing code, you can find me playing my mandolin, building electric guitars, or chasing my 2 year old daughter around the house. I love maps, single-malt scotch, audiobooks. I really love the Fighting Illini, my family, and classical music, although not necessarily in that order.

Finally, I'm a maker. I love to create and build. I always have. This is what drew me to web development in the first place. Whether the medium is wood, music, Legos, or code; I'm happiest when I'm creating something that challenges me.

About this project

For most of the year I've been teaching myself to code. I have mostly been using tutorial-based online resources like Codecademy and Code School, or challenge-based tools like Code Wars. Those sites are great, but there's a limit to how useful they are. I'm not aware of any great market demand for tutorial-completers. I was getting good and tutorials but still not good at making anything useful. My learning was moving too slowly.

I decided I needed to adopt the JFDI method and actually build something. I have been kicking a few ideas around in my head for far too long and it's time I try them out. I have a few simple rules for myself:

  • Complete (and release) one new website every week.
  • Learn and use a new skill with each weekly website.
  • Document what I learn in a weekly blog.
  • Every site should be useful.

That's it. When I head to Chicago in June, I will have 24 websites under my belt. You can follow my progress here and on my blog. Thanks for stopping by!
